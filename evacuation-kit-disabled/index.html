Prepare your Emergency Kit

Do you have an emergency kit on hand?

Mobility limitations may make it difficult for a person to use stairs or to move quickly over long distances. Limitations may include reliance on mobility equipment such as a wheelchair, walker, crutches or a walking cane. People with a heart condition or respiratory difficulties may also have limited mobility.

BASIC KIT CHECKLIST

A whistle (in case you need help)

Garbage Bag

Water purifying tablet

Cash or change for pay phones

RECOMMENDED ADDITIONAL ITEMS

Tire patch kit

Can of seal-in-air product (to repair flat tires 

Supply of inner tubes 

Latex-free gloves (for anyone providing personal care to you)

Spare deep-cycle battery for a motorized wheelchair or scooter

A lightweight, manual wheelchair as a backup to a motorized wheelchair (if feasible)

Spare catheters (if applicable)

Your power outage backup plan

KNOW YOUR EMERGENCY PLAN

  • If you are using a wheelchair or scooter, request that an emergency evacuation chair be placed near a stairwell on the same floor where you are working or staying, so that it can be quickly reached by your network to help you evacuate. The person with the disability should be involved in the selection of the evacuation chair.
  • People who need an evacuation chair to be used should appoint a primary and backup contact to assist them in an evacuation case. Establish an evacuation plan and practise using the chair with the building manager and the contact persons in cooperation.
  • Identify areas of your body that have decreased sensation in your medical evaluation checklist so that in an emergency these areas can be tested for injuries, if you can not do so yourself.
  • Check with your local municipal office to find out if there are wheelchair accessible emergency shelters in your nearby.
