Be aware of these emergency signs

During an emergency, people can respond in different ways. Some may stand still and freeze in their place.

Fire Safety Signs can help greatly during an emergency because the signs will not only help occupants to know where to go or what to do – the signs can also help leaders and fire marshals to locate the right equipment to fight the fire.

FIRE HOSE

FIRE HOSE signs indicate that there is a fire hose so as to make people aware in the event of discovering a fire or upon hearing the alarm

FIRE EXTINGUISHER

Quickly and easily alert people to the location of fire exits, escapes, hoses, and extinguishers with our range of fire service sign

EMERGENCY TELEPHONE NUMBER

An Emergency Telephone Sign uses safety messages and symbols for emergency response protocol and compliance. An Emergency Telephone Sign identifies the location of an emergency telephone system in case of a fire emergency

FIRE CALL POINTS

These signs are installed to instantly show occupants of a commercial or publically accessed building the location of fire equipment, such as fire alarms, fire extinguishers, fire hoses and emergency stop buttons.

FIRE BLANKETS

Fire blankets can be used to extinguish small fires in the home, caravan, boat or garage. They can also be used to wrap around a person whose clothes have caught alight.

FIRE FIGHTING EQUIPMENT

Fire-fighting Equipment Location Signs show the location of fire-fighting equipment such as extinguishers

DO NOT USE LIFT

Fire exit signs are used to clearly inform occupants about not to use elevators in case of fire. USE STAIRWAY

FIRE EXIT

Fire exit signs are used to clearly inform occupants of a building of the nearest emergency exits, safe evacuation route and general evacuation procedures. They can also offer detailed instructions, such as how to open doorways.

REFUGE POINT

Disability signs are used to indicate access or special provisions for disabled people. Refuge signs indicate the presence of a refuge point

FIRST AID

Used to mark and distinguish where first aid equipment is located, these signs offer simple instructions. They can also give more detailed information, such as who the relevant first-aider is and emergency instructions for people with disabilities

EMERGENCY EVACUATION CHAIR

This is a proposed sign for refuge areas and other areas that have Emergency Evacuation Chairs. It incorporates the use of the Accessible Means of Egress Icon Design and represents a person being evacuated using an evacuation chair, whilst another person using a wheelchair is waiting in a Refuge Area (i.e. the enlarged fire stair landing).

SAFE REFUGE POINT

Safe refuge point wheelchair / disabled symbol with location and space to write – where the address of safe spot will be mentioned
