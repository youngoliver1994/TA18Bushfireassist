Prepare your home

In a bushfire, well-prepared houses have a higher chance of surviving. Even if you plan to leave early, be more prepared for the house so that the more likely it is to survive a bushfire or ember attack. If the fire breaks out suddenly and you cannot leave, a well-prepared house will provide you with more protection.

Here are some basic maintenance tips to prepare your house:

1.Clear and maintain gutters of leaves, twigs and rubbish.

2.Ensure underfloor areas are enclosed or screened.

3.Seal all gaps, vents and roof spaces to prevent embers

4.entering your house.

5.Store fuels and chemicals away from your house.

6.Store LPG gas tanks appropriately. They should be vented away from your house.

7.Move woodpiles away from the house.

 

 

Vegetation Maintenance tips:

 

1.Clear fine fuels from around your home (fine fuels are those that are the same thickness or less than a pencil, such as grass, bark and leaves).

2.Keep grass areas well trimmed and watered

3.Grass should be no more than 10 centimeters high within 30 meters of your home.

4.Rake and clear leaf litter (dead leaves) regularly.

5.Leaf litter must be no more than one centimeters in depth.

6.Remove flammable mulch from around your house.

7.It is extremely dangerous if used within 10 meters of your home, especially under windows.

8.Remove or trim shrubs. There should be no shrubs over one meter next to or below windows.

9.Trim tree branches overhanging your house.

 

Other tips:

 

1.Ensure you have adequate home and contents insurance.

2.List your irreplaceable family keepsakes and valuables.

3.Identify a safe location to store these valuables. Where will you locate them?

4.Consider moving these out of the area during summer.

5.Ensure access to your property is easy for fire vehicles.

6.Check that pumps, generators and water systems are working correctly
