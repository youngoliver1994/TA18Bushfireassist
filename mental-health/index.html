Manage your Mental Health

Managing your mental health in the event of a bushfire is essential to ensure that you keep resilient in times of disaster. Here you’ll find tips on how to build better mental health.

Steps to follow to look after your mental health

Step 1

Spend time with your family and friends

Step 3

Continue a healthy lifestyle (try to eat well, sleep and exercise)

Step 5

Accept help when it’s offered

Step 2

Try to get back to a routine but don’t push yourself and work too hard

Step 4

Take time out, but don’t isolate yourself

Step 6

Limit the amount of media coverage you see and hear

Keep Yourself Aware

Who gets affected

While looking after yourself, it is also vital to look after your family and friends, and to get help when necessary. Vulnerable individuals include those who are:

  • Elderly and/or isolated
  • Having a pre-existing mental disorder
  • Having a history of trauma
What the symptoms are

Emotions that you may experience in the event of a bushfire include:

  • Fear
  • Anxiety and/or panic
  • Insomnia
  • Distress
  • Inability to focus
How to deal with the emotional impact of bushfire
  • Spend time with your loved ones
  • Try to maintain a routine to keep yourself busy
  • Keep yourself calm by doing things you enjoy
  • Vocalise your feelings when you are ready
  • Reward yourself whenever you notice improvement

CALL for assistance

There are several options available for you if you require support and/or counselling:

  • In the event of a public health emergency, you can call the Public Health Information Hotline between 8:30am and 5pm AEST/AEDT on weekdays at 1800 004 599
