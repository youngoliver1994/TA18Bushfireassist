Returning Home

Want to return home?

After experiencing a bushfire, you may have been evacuated or unable to go home. You may want to go back but the bushfire is likely to have caused some damage to your house or the area your house is located in. Many preparations must be made before you decide to return home. The following content provides some questions to consider before returning.

Dangerous materials to be aware of:

FIRST SORT

+ Ashes
+ Dust

THIRD SORT

+ LP gas cylinders
+ Remnants of charred appliances

Second sort

+ Asbestos
+ Chemicals such as chlorine and ammonia

Contact Emergency Service

If you find any of these materials in or around your home, please contact your local emergency service department for advice.

Things you should know about returning home:

What you might experience in your surroundings
  • Burnt buildings and vehicles
  • Blackened areas
  • Ash-covered zones
  • Burnt smells
  • Missing landmarks
Some things you need to consider when getting ready to return home
  • If your vehicle has enough fuel
  • If you have sufficient food and water
  • If the roads are safe to drive/walk on
  • If you are wearing appropriate, protective clothing
  • If your phone has sufficient battery and balance
Some things you need to consider when you have returned home
  • Practice caution upon entering your home or other areas until it has been officially confirmed that it is safe to do so
  • Ring the relevant services if you need advice on operating your water, gas and electricity supplies
  • Ring the relevant services if you need disposal advice in the presence of any dangerous materials aforementioned
  • Ring the relevant services if you find broken electrical cables and wires
  • Discard food and water that may have been contaminated
  • Be aware that a flare up may occur at any given moment so practice vigilance and keep informed always
