Prepare your Property

Why to Prepare Your Property?

Everyone who lives near dense forest, bush, grassland or the coast needs to prepare their property for bushfire. Good preparation for your house will be helpful for reducing the risk of property loss and the risk of life-threatening harm when a Bushfire occur. If the fire breaks out suddenly and you cannot leave, a well-prepared house will provide you with more protection.

Vegetation maintenance

Reducing the vegetation around your home is one of the most important things you can do to keep your home safe in a bushfire.

House ImPROVEMENTS

Anywhere embers can lodge or enter your house can start a fire. To reduce the impact of embers on your home there are some important building improvements that are recommended.

STORAGE MANAGEMENT

Improper management of flammable item might be more harmful to your property. Rational storage can minimize the risk in advance.

Regular inspection

Inspecting your property regularly ensures all the maintenance are carried out correctly to eliminate potential risks.

property preparedness tips

How to Keep Your Garden Bushfire Free?
  • Clear fine fuels from around your home (fine fuels are those that are the same thickness or less than a pencil, such as grass, bark and leaves).
  • Keep grass areas well trimmed and watered.
  • Grass should be no more than 10 centimetres high within 30 metres of your home.
  • Remove flammable mulch from around your house.
  • It is extremely dangerous if used within 10 metres of your home, especially under windows.
Know How Dangerous a Leaf Could Be
  • Grass should be no more than 10 centimetres high within 30 metres of your home.
  • Rake and clear leaf litter (dead leaves) regularly.
  • Leaf litter must be no more than one centimetre in depth.
  • Remove or trim shrubs. There should be no shrubs over one metre next to or below windows.
  • Trim tree branches overhanging your house.
How to store your Items?
  • Store fuels and chemicals away from your house.
  • Store LPG gas tanks appropriately. They should be vented away from your house.
  • Move woodpiles away from the house.
  • List your irreplaceable family keepsakes and valuables.
  • Identify a safe location to store these valuables. 
  • Consider moving the valuables out of the area during summer.
  • Ensure there are no things that block your house, and access to it is easy for fire vehicles.
